INPUT DEVICE

Definition:  Input devices are hardware components used to send data, commands, or signals to the computer for processing. They act as the bridge between the user and the machine.
Examples:  Keyboard, mouse, microphone, scanner, and touchscreens.
Functions:  Allow users to provide instructions to the computer. Capture raw data from the real world and convert it into a digital format. Importance: Without input devices, the computer cannot receive instructions or perform tasks as per user needs.

OUTPUT DEVICE

Definition:  Output devices display or present the processed data from the computer to the user in a readable or usable format.
Examples:  Monitor, printer, speakers, and projectors.
Functions:  Translate the computer's digital signals into visual, auditory, or physical outputs. Provide feedback or results of the computer's operations to the user.
Importance:   These devices enable the user to interact with and benefit from the computer's processing capabilities
